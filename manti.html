Manti

Description

This is a recipe of Manti, a traditional dish from turkey, my home country.

It is traditionally served with joghurt and a tomato butter sauce.

Actually it is a very simple dish, all you need is a little time. Because you have to prepare the dough and let it rest at least an hour or two. Afte that is done you need to form little balls and open them up so you can fill it with the ground beef mixtutre. After all that you must throw them in boiling water with a bit of salt and let them boil at least ten minutes. After that you can start preparing the tomato-butter sauce, all you need to do is to add butter in a pan as much you prefer and ad tomato paste to it and let it cook for a bit until its a mass.

Manti recipe

Ingredients

    For the dough:

  • 500 g flour
  • 1 Egg
  • 1 Tsp salt

    For the filling:

  • 250 g ground beef
  • 1 onion
  • 2 Tsp parsley, chopped
  • salt, pepper and Paprika powder

    For the sauce:

  • 750 g joghurt
  • 3 Garlic cloves
  • 3 Tbs of Butter
  • 2 Tbs of tomato past
  • paprika powder

Steps

    Preparation of the dough:

  1. Give the ingredients al in big Bowl and knead everything until the dough is solid. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes to 1 houhr.

    2. The filling:

  2. The onions have to be choped very finely and mixed with the other ingredients.

  3. Now you have to split the dough in two parts and roll it out until its 2 mm thick and cut it in squers a 2 x 2 cm and fill them with the beef filling. Press the opposite corners of the squares together to form a cross at the top and place them on a floured tray.

  4. Bring salted water to the boil and cook the dumplings in it for about 10 minutes over medium heat.

    5. The sauce:

  5. Finally chop the garlic cloves, add to the yoghurt with a pinch of salt and stir. Heat the butter in a pan and briefly fry the tomato paste and pepper powder in it.

  6. Using a slotted spoon, distribute the dumplings directly onto the plates and allow to cool slightly. Pour the yoghurt on top and finally drizzle the paprika tomato butter over the mantis.
